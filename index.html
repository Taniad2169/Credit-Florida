Credit Repair Legal Disclaimers & Required Notices

Federal law requires that we provide you with these important disclosures about credit repair services:

CONSUMER CREDIT FILE RIGHTS UNDER STATE AND FEDERAL LAW

You have a right to dispute inaccurate information in your credit report by contacting the credit bureau directly. However, neither you nor a credit repair company has the right to have accurate, current, and verifiable information removed from your credit report. The credit bureau must remove accurate, negative information from your report only if it is over 7 years old. Bankruptcy information can be reported for 10 years.

Important: You have a right to obtain a copy of your credit report from a credit bureau. You may be charged a reasonable fee. There is no fee, however, if you have been turned down for credit, employment, insurance, or a rental dwelling because of information in your credit report within the preceding 60 days.

YOUR RIGHTS UNDER THE CREDIT REPAIR ORGANIZATIONS ACT

  • You have the right to cancel your contract with any credit repair organization for any reason within 3 business days from the date you signed it.
  • Credit repair organizations may not charge you until they have completed the services they have promised.
  • Some credit repair organizations promise more than they can deliver. They may claim they can remove accurate negative information from your credit report. This is not true.
  • You have the right to sue a credit repair organization that violates the Credit Repair Organization Act.
  • Any waiver by you of your rights under the Credit Repair Organizations Act is void and unenforceable.

FEDERAL TRADE COMMISSION NOTICE

The Federal Trade Commission (FTC) regulates credit repair organizations. You may contact the FTC at 600 Pennsylvania Avenue NW, Washington, DC 20580, or by phone at 1-877-FTC-HELP (1-877-382-4357).

STATE LAW NOTICES

Many states have laws regulating credit repair companies. Your state Attorney General's office or local consumer protection agency may have more information about your rights under state law.

Notice: By proceeding with this application, you acknowledge that you have read and understand these legal disclaimers. We do not guarantee specific results, and we cannot remove accurate negative information from your credit report.
